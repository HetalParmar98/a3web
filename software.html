Software we use

ArcGIS Pro

ArcGIS Pro is a versatile tool that caters to a wide range of industries, including urban planning, environmental science, natural resource management, public health, emergency response, and more. Professionals leverage its capabilities to solve spatial problems, make informed decisions, and communicate spatial information effectively.

Adobe illustrator

GIS professionals often use Adobe Illustrator in conjunction with GIS software, such as ArcGIS, to create visually appealing and customized maps for various purposes. By combining the spatial analysis capabilities of GIS software with the design and illustration features of Adobe Illustrator, GIS professionals can create visually compelling maps that effectively communicate complex spatial information to a wide audience.

Catalyst professional

CATALYST Professional is the world's leading geospatial desktop suite, specializing in Remote Sensing, Photogrammetry & Earth Observation Science for optical, and SAR imagery. Easily integrate our powerful image analysis, ortho-mosaicking, and image processing capabilities into your geospatial technology stack.

Microsoft Access

Microsoft Access can be used to store and manage non-spatial attribute data associated with geographic features. This data can include information such as attribute values, names, and other non-spatial details.

AutoCAD

AutoCAD supports the import of GIS data in various formats, such as Shapefiles or GeoJSON. This allows GIS professionals to bring in existing spatial data into AutoCAD for further editing or analysis.

Global Mapper

Global Mapper supports a wide range of spatial data formats, allowing GIS professionals to import data from sources such as Shapefiles, KML/KMZ, GeoTIFF, and many others.
