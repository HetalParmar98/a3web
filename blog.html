GIS Trends 2023:Navigating the Currents of Saptial Technology

The GIS landscape for 2023 is marked by several key trends and advancements. Gartner predicts a significant realization of wireless value in geospatial applications, particularly in GeoAI and automated geospatial feature extraction. Esri's ArcGIS Pro introduces notable updates, including 3D mapping enhancements, indoor positioning tools, and the long-awaited Time Enabled Scene Layers for temporal data visualization. QGIS 3.30 focuses on mesh editing, processing script templates, and dissolve geometries, enhancing its versatility. PostGIS 3.4 drops support for older versions and introduces ST Transform for flexible geospatial transformations. Mapbox emphasizes Geocoding v6, mobile map development, and 3D capabilities, indicating a push for immersive spatial experiences. The overarching theme suggests a growing emphasis on 3D applications, automation, and risk mitigation in geospatial technologies, anticipating exciting surprises and developments in the dynamic GIS landscape. .

Mapping science

The National Science Foundation's (NSF) investment 35 years ago played a pivotal role in shaping the field of geographic information sciences (GIS), which has become integral to modern life. GIS, initially known as spatial data handling, emerged as a technical pursuit in the 1950s and 60s, focusing on using computers to manage and analyze spatial data. Over time, the Environmental Systems Research Institute (Esri) played a significant role in transforming GIS into a business by selling software developed for land-use and planning projects. In the 1980s, debates arose about whether GIS was more than just a mapping technology, leading to the establishment of the National Center for Geographic Information and Analysis (NCGIA) in 1988 with NSF funding. Today, GIS is ubiquitous, influencing industries, scientific research, and various disciplines, with the NSF continuing its support through numerous grants totaling over $83 million in the last two years alone.Read full article.
