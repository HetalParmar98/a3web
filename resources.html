Data collection, management, and analysis are facilitated by the computerized framework known as geographic information systems (GIS) technology. It is based on the science of geography and combines robust data integration, analysis, and modeling framework with potent mapping and visualization. GIS integrates a wide range of data types and has scientific roots. Information is arranged into layers on geographic displays, such maps, using spatial location. GIS's special ability allows it to offer deeper insights into data, including correlations, trends, and situations, which in turn helps users make more informed decisions. Making judgments is aided by having access to maps. That is the main purpose of GIS.
